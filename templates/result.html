{% elif prediction == "Nitrogen" %}
N

What is Nitrogen Deficiency?

Nitrogen is essential for plant growth and chlorophyll production. Deficiency typically shows as yellowing of older leaves first, starting from the tips and edges.

Quick Actions

  • • Apply nitrogen-rich fertilizer (NPK with higher N value)
  • • Use organic compost or manure
  • • Consider foliar spray for quick absorption
  • • Monitor soil pH (optimal: 6.0-7.0)
{% elif prediction == "Phosphorus" %}
P

What is Phosphorus Deficiency?

Phosphorus is crucial for energy transfer and root development. Deficiency often causes dark green or purplish discoloration on leaves and stunted growth.

Quick Actions

  • • Use phosphorus-rich fertilizer (higher P in NPK)
  • • Add bone meal to the soil
  • • Ensure soil pH is correct (5.5-6.5)
  • • Avoid excessive watering
{% elif prediction == "Potassium" %}
K

What is Potassium Deficiency?

Potassium regulates water and nutrient movement. Deficiency shows as brown, scorched-looking edges on leaves and weak stems.

Quick Actions

  • • Apply potassium-rich fertilizer (higher K in NPK)
  • • Use wood ash or kelp meal
  • • Ensure proper drainage
  • • Monitor for pest damage
